Permanent Makeup FAQ

1. What is permanent makeup?
Permanent makeup (also known as cosmetic tattooing or micropigmentation) is a technique where pigments are implanted into the skin to create the appearance of makeup. It can be used for eyebrows, eyeliner, lips, and more, enhancing your natural features.
2. How long does permanent makeup last?
The results of permanent makeup typically last 1-3 years, depending on factors like skin type, lifestyle, and how well you take care of the treated area. Touch-ups are recommended to maintain the desired look over time.
3. Does it hurt?
The discomfort level varies by person, but most clients report minimal pain during the procedure. Numbing creams are applied to ensure you remain comfortable throughout the process.

Microblading uses a handheld tool to create hair-like strokes on the skin, while nano blading uses a digital machine with fine needles for more precise, long-lasting hair strokes. Nano blading is suitable for all skin types and tends to last longer.
5. Is there any downtime?
Permanent makeup procedures generally have minimal downtime. You may experience some swelling and redness immediately after, but this usually subsides within a few days. The treated area may also appear darker for a week or two before softening to the final shade.
6. Can I still wear regular makeup?
Absolutely! Permanent makeup enhances your natural look, but you can still apply regular makeup to achieve different styles or bolder looks.
7. Who is a good candidate for permanent makeup?
Permanent makeup is ideal for anyone who wants to enhance their natural features, save time on their daily beauty routine, or has difficulty applying traditional makeup due to vision issues, shaky hands, or allergies to conventional products.

During the first few days, the color will appear more intense and may feel slightly swollen. You may experience light flaking as the area heals. Itâ€™s important to follow aftercare instructions closely, avoid picking at the treated area, and keep it moisturized.
10. How soon can I schedule a touch-up?
A touch-up is usually recommended 6-8 weeks after the initial procedure to perfect the results and ensure the pigment has settled evenly.

Lash Lift and Brow Lamination FAQ

1. What is a lash lift and tint?
A lash lift is a semi-permanent treatment that curls your natural lashes from the base, making them appear longer and more lifted. A lash tint is added to enhance the color, making the lashes look darker and fuller.
2. How long does a lash lift and tint last?
A lash lift typically lasts 6-8 weeks, depending on your lash growth cycle. The tint usually lasts about 4-6 weeks, gradually fading as your lashes shed and regrow.
3. Is the lash lift treatment safe for my eyes?
Yes, the lash lift is a gentle process. The products are formulated for the delicate eye area, and the procedure is performed with your eyes closed for comfort. Your technician is trained to ensure your safety during the treatment.

5. How should I care for my lashes after a lash lift and tint?
For the first 24 hours, avoid getting your lashes wet, using makeup, or exposing them to steam or heat. After that, you can resume your regular skincare and makeup routine. Avoid waterproof mascara, as it can weaken the lift over time.
6. Will a lash lift damage my natural lashes?
No, a lash lift does not damage your natural lashes when done correctly by a trained professional. Proper aftercare and following maintenance guidelines will keep your lashes healthy.
7. What is brow lamination and tint?
Brow lamination is a non-invasive treatment that smooths and lifts your eyebrow hairs, creating a fuller, more defined look. Brow tint is added to enhance the color and make the brows appear more uniform and bold.
8. How long does brow lamination and tint last?
Brow lamination can last up to 6-8 weeks with proper aftercare. The tint typically lasts 4-6 weeks, gradually fading as your brows shed and grow.

10. How do I care for my brows after lamination?
For the first 24 hours, avoid getting your brows wet or using any harsh products. Afterward, keep your brows moisturized with nourishing oils or creams to prolong the effect. Avoid rubbing or scrubbing the area.
